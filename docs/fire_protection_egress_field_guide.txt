FIRE PROTECTION & EGRESS — FIELD REFERENCE GUIDE
Flynn Group of Companies | Document No: FG-FP-FIELD-2024 | Rev 1.0 | March 2024
================================================================================

PURPOSE
This field guide is a quick reference for Flynn site supervisors, foremen, and
trade workers. It summarizes key fire protection and egress requirements from
FG-FP-2024 for on-site use. Always refer to the full technical standard and
project-specific drawings for complete requirements.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 1 — FIRE RESISTANCE RATINGS AT A GLANCE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

EXTERIOR WALLS (non-combustible construction):
  - Building height > 18 m, Group A/B occupancy:   2-hour minimum FRR
  - Sprinklered building (with AHJ approval):       Rating may be reduced 1 step
  - Curtain wall spandrel panels at floor lines:    1-hour minimum FRR
  - Glazing in fire-rated assemblies:               Must be fire-rated glass (ULC listed)

ROOF ASSEMBLIES (ASTM E108 / ULC-S107 classification required):
  - Within 3 m of property line or adjacent structure:   Class A required
  - Industrial occupancies, setback > 3 m:               Class B acceptable
  - All other commercial projects (minimum):             Class C

IMPORTANT: Verify FM Approvals listing number for all TPO, PVC, and modified
bitumen assemblies before material submittal.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 2 — FIRESTOPPING QUICK REFERENCE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

WHEN IS FIRESTOPPING REQUIRED?
Every penetration through a fire-rated assembly must be firestopped, including:
  - Mechanical, electrical, and plumbing penetrations
  - Structural penetrations (bolts, anchors, hangers)
  - Curtain wall-to-slab interface gaps (linear joints)

CURTAIN WALL FLOOR LINE DETAILS:
  - Install ULC-listed safing insulation (minimum 4 pcf density)
  - Minimum depth: 4 inches (100 mm)
  - Apply intumescent sealant at interior face
  - F-rating must equal or exceed the floor assembly FRR

DOCUMENTATION REQUIRED:
  - Complete Form FG-FS-001 (Firestop Inspection Record) for EVERY location
  - Photograph each installation before concealment
  - Records must be retained for the life of the building

COMMON FIELD MISTAKES TO AVOID:
  1. Using the wrong firestop system for the penetrant type — always verify ULC listing
  2. Insufficient safing depth — measure before closing up
  3. Missing intumescent sealant at interior face
  4. Not documenting locations — this creates liability at closeout
  5. Firestopping after drywall is closed — schedule inspections proactively

SECTION 3 — MEANS OF EGRESS REQUIREMENTS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

EXIT DOOR BASICS:
  - Occupant load >= 50: Door must swing in direction of travel
  - Occupant load >= 100 (assembly): Panic hardware (push bar) required
  - Minimum clear width (single leaf): 810 mm
  - Minimum clear width (accessible, double leaf): 1500 mm
  - Hardware: Operable with single motion, no special knowledge required

MAXIMUM TRAVEL DISTANCES TO EXIT:
  Occupancy Group          | Unsprinklered | Sprinklered
  -------------------------|---------------|------------
  A — Assembly             | 45 m          | 60 m
  B — Care / Detention     | 30 m          | 45 m
  D/E — Business/Mercantile| 40 m          | 60 m
  F — Industrial           | 45 m          | 75 m

Note: Travel distance measured from most remote point in floor area to nearest
exit door, along the natural path of egress travel.

WHEN ARE TWO EXITS REQUIRED?
  - Floor area > 60 m² AND occupant load > 60 persons (Group A)
  - Floor area > 60 m² AND occupant load > 150 persons (Group B/D)
  - Any floor area in a building > 18 m building height

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 4 — EMERGENCY LIGHTING & EXIT SIGNAGE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

EMERGENCY LIGHTING:
  - Minimum illuminance: 10 lux at floor level along egress path
  - Minimum duration: 30 minutes after power failure
  - Power source: Battery backup (or generator with automatic transfer)

EXIT SIGNS:
  - Must be illuminated (internal or external light source)
  - Minimum luminance: 50 candela/m²
  - Battery backup: Minimum 30-minute operation

PHOTOLUMINESCENT MARKINGS (high-rise buildings > 18 m):
  - Required in all exit corridors and stairwells
  - Installed at floor level
  - Must meet CAN/ULC-S572

TESTING REQUIREMENTS:
  - Monthly test: 30 seconds (document on Form FG-EL-003)
  - Annual test: 30 minutes full duration (document on Form FG-EL-003)

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 5 — SPRINKLER COORDINATION CHECKLIST
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Before Flynn work begins, confirm with sprinkler contractor:

[ ] All concealed spaces > 800 mm depth have concealed-space sprinklers
[ ] Sprinkler head locations documented and coordinated with insulation scope
[ ] Floor-line sprinklers behind spandrel panels installed before panel installation
[ ] Thermal barrier not compromised at sprinkler penetrations
[ ] Minimum 0.07 MPa residual pressure confirmed at most remote head
[ ] Hydraulic calculations reviewed and approved by AHJ

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SECTION 6 — SMOKE & HEAT DETECTOR COORDINATION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

SMOKE DETECTORS REQUIRED IN:
  - All plenum spaces formed by Flynn curtain wall or ceiling systems
  - Concealed cavity spaces (spacing per NBC 2020 and manufacturer listings)

HEAT DETECTORS (instead of smoke) IN:
  - Parking structures
  - Loading dock areas
  - Kitchen exhaust zones adjacent to building envelope penetrations

BUILDING ENVELOPE CONTRACTOR RESPONSIBILITY:
  - Provide rough-in conduit per electrical drawings in curtain wall framing
  - Notify fire protection engineer of all plenum and concealed space locations
  - Do not close concealed spaces until detector installation is confirmed complete
